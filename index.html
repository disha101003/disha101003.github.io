03 — Research

Academic Work

Bayesian Deep Learning

Professor Ruqi Zhang, Junbo Li Jun 2024 – Jul 2025

I developed SDSI, a variational inference framework that restricts posterior variances to a sparse, low-dimensional subspace, enabling scalable Bayesian neural networks with improved efficiency. The method introduces a subspace addition strategy that preserves high-gradient directions, resulting in enhanced predictive accuracy and calibration across both clean and corrupted datasets.

SDSI achieved 76.36% accuracy, 0.9655 AUROC, and 0.0014 ECE on CIFAR-100 (10% subnet), outperforming Sparse BNNs, VBLL approaches, and Laplace Subnetworks. The framework reaches full BNN-level predictive performance in only 40 training epochs.

Continual Learning

Oct 2023 – Jun 2024
First Author ReScience C · Accepted

"[Re] BiRT: Bio-Inspired Replay in Vision Transformers"

I conducted a reproducibility study on BiRT, a continual learning framework that leverages vision transformers with constructive noise and episodic memory replay to mitigate catastrophic forgetting. The project involved re-implementing the method, evaluating performance across multiple benchmarks, and analyzing the robustness and practical applicability of the approach. Published in ReScience C.

Special thanks to Jinen Setpal and ML@Purdue for their support and resources.

Neuromorphic Computing

Dr. Prasanna Date, Dr. Shruti Kulkarni · Oak Ridge National Laboratory May 2023 – Aug 2023
First Author IEEE ICRC · Accepted

"An FPGA-Based Neuromorphic Processor with All-to-All Connectivity"

I developed a preliminary implementation of an FPGA-based neuromorphic processor featuring all-to-all connectivity on an Artix-7 FPGA. Implemented Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) in Verilog on the Basys3 board using Xilinx Vivado, validated architecture on algorithmic test cases, and designed custom IP modules including UART transceivers, receivers, and FIFO buffers at 9600 bps.
